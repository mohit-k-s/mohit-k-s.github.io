I never thought i have to ever write about this topic, This again is maybe going to be a bit long because the underlying issue is so deep rooted in our current society that it has become impossible to upheave it. 

Let's start with a story , suppose there is a community which are mostly inter-dependent then there can most likely be a conflict between them because dependence leads to a sense of feeling that they can manipulate what they cost others. Now in this community everyone has same wealth , even then there is a perfect good chance that there will be a conflict. So what do people do ? They need someone else to resolve it , if they want to do no harm to each other physically and arrive at a possible solution amicably. 

This new someone is made aware that they are very important yet they are meant to serve the people in case of such issues. Now some people start demanding that this person maybe should also look after the management of the infrastructure of their economy so they can focus on maybe themselves ?
Now the person demands that they cannot manage the infra on their own , so they take money from other people to do this job. This is what is called tax.
People did ask this person that they should be accountable to that money and people should get the benefits back as well. Happily agreed.

One time this person spent on a post office box , other time on reparing a road, times were good. 

<i>Enter Greed</i>

This person now realises that his role is actually not to serve the people but to **rule** them. Thoughts like , hey maybe i can manipulate the cost of this new thing and demand more in the next cycle and cite this. And in next cycle be ready with another activity that he wants to do that requires pre-charging. 

Since this community was small , people beat the sense to him and removed him from this position and brought someone else. The next one did the similar thing and this time he hired a *muscle* privately for his protection. This *muscle* beat men attacking him. But people still were able to beat both of them.
